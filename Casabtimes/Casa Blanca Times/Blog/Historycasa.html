    History of Casablanca

    The history of Casablanca goes back many centuries, as it was originally a small fishing village located on the Atlantic coast of Morocco. Over time, the city witnessed great development until it became one of the largest modern and prosperous cities in the Kingdom of Morocco.

    Casablanca was founded as a scientific settlement from It was accepted by the Portuguese in the 15th century, and continued under their authority until 1755 when a strong earthquake destroyed the city. Then, control passed to the Kingdom of Morocco and Casablanca turned into a major commercial city and economic center.

    During the nineteenth century, Casablanca witnessed... It expanded significantly and turned into a modern city with developed infrastructure and amazing buildings. The city was designed according to the vision of Frenchman Albert Beck, who saw in Casablanca an opportunity to create an ideal modern city with unique architecture.

    The development and growth of Casablanca continued in the following decades, It has become a major economic capital of Morocco and a business and trade center. Today, the city of Casablanca is one of the most prominent tourist destinations in Morocco, as visitors from all over the world come to explore its ancient history and charming landmarks.

    Weather in Casablanca

    If you are planning to visit Casablanca, it is important to take into account the weather during your trip. Casablanca enjoys a pleasant Mediterranean climate throughout the year, making it a favorite tourist destination in Morocco.

    The best time to visit Casablanca is during the spring (from March to May) and autumn (September to November), when temperatures are mild and rain is sparse. You can enjoy nice sunny days and spend time exploring the sights.

    In the summer (June to August), the temperatures in Casablanca are very high, as It can reach about 30 degrees Celsius or more. This may be a good time to enjoy sandy beaches and water activities, but it is recommended to take preventive measures to protect from the strong sun rays and maintain hydration.

    As for the winter (from December to February). ), rainfall is greater in Casablanca. Rainfall amounts may range between 50-100 mm per month. You can wear warm clothes, prepare yourself for the humid weather conditions, and enjoy the beautiful, refreshing sea views.

    Casablanca is the economic capital

    Casablanca is considered one of the most prominent economic cities in Morocco, playing an important role in promoting trade and business in the country. Many major companies and global headquarters are located in Casablanca, reflecting its urban development and involvement in the global economy. Casablanca is known for its many thriving industrial and commercial regions that attract investments and support the economic growth of the city and the country in general. The Grand Port of Casablanca is considered one of the most important seaports in the region, as many products and goods are exported and imported to and from all over the world.

    International Trade

    Casablanca enjoys a distinct geographical location on the Atlantic coast, which facilitates the process of trade and economic exchange with many countries. There are many major complexes and commercial centers in the city, attracting many traders and investors from all over the world. In addition, Casablanca has a modern infrastructure and advanced facilities to facilitate companies’ operations and enhance the economic sustainability of the region.

    Main economic sectors

    Casablanca is experiencing strong growth in several key economic sectors. The services sector tops the list, with many international companies in the areas of insurance, banking, consulting, and technology. The industrial sector also plays an important role in the local economy, including automobile, textile, and food manufacturing. The construction and real estate sector also constitutes a strategic sector in Casablanca, as the city is witnessing significant urban expansion and development in infrastructure and real estate projects.
