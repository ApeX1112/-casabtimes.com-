    Casablanca Chess 2024: A Grand Celebration of Chess and Innovation

    The Casablanca Chess 2024 event is set to be a remarkable celebration of chess, combining tradition with modern innovation. Organized by the Casablanca Stock Exchange, Casa Events & Animation, and the Royal Moroccan Chess Federation, the tournament will take place in the vibrant city of Casablanca, Morocco, from May 17 to 19, 2024.

    Tournament Highlights

    Elite Players

    Four of the world's top grandmasters will participate in this event, representing four continents:

    • Magnus Carlsen (Europe): Former World Chess Champion and current world #1.
    • Hikaru Nakamura (North America): Renowned chess streamer and world #3.
    • Viswanathan Anand (Asia): Five-time World Chess Champion and a pioneering figure in Indian chess.
    • Amin Bassem (Africa): The strongest African grandmaster and multiple-time continental champion.

    Casablanca Chess Variant

    A unique aspect of this tournament is the introduction of the "Casablanca Chess Variant." This variant involves starting games from historically significant positions chosen by grandmasters Hicham Hamdouchi and Laurent Fressinet. The positions are selected to ensure balanced and rich possibilities for both players, fostering creativity and exciting gameplay.

    Event Schedule

    The tournament will include rapid games with a time control of 15 minutes plus a 10-second increment per move. The event will be broadcast live on chess.com, providing global access to the matches. Each round will be preceded by a segment dedicated to the selection of the starting position, adding an interactive element to the event.

    Morocco Chess Week

    Casablanca Chess 2024 is part of the larger Morocco Chess Week, which features a variety of chess-related activities aimed at promoting the game among people of all ages and backgrounds. The festival includes masterclasses, conferences, workshops, and community outreach programs. Highlights of the schedule include:

    • May 17: Official opening ceremony and a conference on the history of chess.
    • May 18: Torch ceremony and the start of the Super Tournament.
    • May 19: Local tournaments, exhibitions, and the awards ceremony.

    FIDE 100 Celebrations

    This year's event coincides with the centenary celebrations of the International Chess Federation (FIDE). As part of these celebrations, Casablanca has been chosen as the African host city. The festivities include the Chess Olympiad Torch Relay, which will travel to Budapest for the Chess Olympiad later this year.

    The Casablanca Chess 2024 promises to be a grand spectacle, honoring the rich history of chess while introducing innovative elements that challenge the creativity and strategic thinking of the world's best players. It is an event that not only celebrates the game but also fosters community and educational values through chess.
